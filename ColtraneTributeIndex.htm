John Coltrane

Pioneer in Free Jazz



Here's a timeline of Coltrane's life:



  • 1926 - Born in Hamlet North Carolina.

  • 1945 - Coltrane inlisted in the Navy.

  • 1946 - Coltrane made his first recordings with the Navy band the Melody Masters. He played jazz standards and bebob tunes on alto saxophone. After he was discharged from the Navy he joined a band led by Jimmy Heath.

  • 1955-1956 - Coltrane joined Miles Davis' quintet and they produced the albums Cookin', Relaxin', Workin', and Steamin'.

  • 1957 - The album Blue Train was produced and contains original compositions by Coltrane and title tracks, "Moments Notice", and "Lazy Bird", both display the chord substitution cycles known as Coltrane changes.

  • 1958 - Coltrane rejoined Davis and his style with this group was described by jazz critic Ira Gitler with the term "sheets of sound."

  • 1959 - Coltrane records Giant Steps which features only his compositions. This album uses Coltrane changes, which are altered chord progression cycles.

  • 1961 - Coltrane recorded the most experimental music he had played thus far. It was influenced by Indian ragas, modal jazz, and fee jazz.

  • 1962 - Coltrane moved toward a more harmonically static style that was spiritually driven.

  • 1964 - The Classic Quartet recorded A Love Supreme a four part suite that was an ode to his love for God.

  • 1965 - Coltrane shows interest in avante garde jazz being produced by musicians like Ornette Coleman and Sun Ra. Coltrane's playing becomes more abstract during this period with the use of multiphonics and altissimo register. His sheets of sound return in new form. Some of the albums recorded are The John Coltrane Quartet Plays, New Thing at Newport, and First Meditations.

  • 1966 - Coltrane led his new group, with Sanders on tenor saxophone, Alice Coltrane on piano, Garrison on bass, and Ali on drums, and they produced new recordings and albums such as Interstellar Space.

  • 1967 - At the age of 40 Coltrane dies of liver cancer.
